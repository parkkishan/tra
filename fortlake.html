Nestled right next to the historic Belgaum Fort, Kote Kore Lake, also known as Kote Keri, is a picturesque and tranquil destination ideal for a family getaway. The lake is set amidst a scenic backdrop of dense, lush forests, creating a serene atmosphere perfect for those seeking a peaceful retreat. Its well-maintained surroundings provide a perfect escape from the hustle and bustle of daily life, offering a blend of natural beauty and calm. The lake’s charm is accentuated by its beautiful waters and the greenery that surrounds it, making it a must-visit spot for nature lovers and families. Whether you're looking to take a leisurely stroll around the lake or simply relax and enjoy the view, this location provides the perfect setting for quality time with loved ones.

What to Expect:


Things to Do at Kote Kore Lake:
Leisurely Walks: Take your time to walk around the serene lake, soaking in the peaceful surroundings and enjoying the fresh air. The walk around the lake offers beautiful views of the water and the lush greenery that surrounds it, making it a perfect spot for relaxation.
Watch the Ducks: The lake is home to several ducks, adding to the charm of the location. You can watch them glide across the water, making for a calm and delightful experience for both children and adults.
Exciting Activities: If you’re in the mood for some adventure, Kote Kore Lake offers various fun activities. You can enjoy pedal boating or speed boating on the lake’s calm waters, or try out other joy rides available on-site. These activities make for a perfect combination of relaxation and fun.
Tips for Visiting:
Open Hours: The lake is open to visitors every day from 4:30 p.m. to 8:30 p.m., offering an evening escape with beautiful sunset views over the water.
Entry Fee: There’s no entry fee to access the lake, making it an affordable spot for everyone to enjoy. However, you will need to pay a fee if you decide to participate in any of the available activities.
The Indian Flag: One of the most striking features of the lake is the giant 110-meter tall Indian flag, which was installed in 2018. It proudly stands on the lake’s banks and has become a major point of interest for visitors, adding to the charm of the location and making it a great spot for photo opportunities.
